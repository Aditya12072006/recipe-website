Dosa

Preparation Time: 40 minutes

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dosa batter
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Potato masala (optional)
  • Chutney for serving

Steps

  1. Place a flat non-stick or cast-iron tawa on medium heat and allow it to heat evenly for 2 to 3 minutes.
  2. Stir the dosa batter well and add a pinch of salt if needed to balance taste.
  3. Lightly grease the tawa with a few drops of oil, then wipe with a cloth or halved onion to spread a thin film.
  4. Pour one ladle of batter in the center and quickly spread it outward in circular motions to make a thin dosa.
  5. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and a few drops on top for even browning.
  6. Cook on medium heat until the top surface dries and the bottom turns golden and crisp.
  7. If using potato masala, place a spoonful in the center once the dosa is cooked.
  8. Fold the dosa in half or roll it gently with a spatula.
  9. Remove and serve immediately with chutney while hot and crisp.
  10. Repeat the process for remaining batter, lightly greasing the tawa between dosas if required.
